What This Is

Africa is not one story.
It never was.

My name is Imani. I am a storyteller.

Africa gave me that. A continent so wide, so ancient, so full of life that one voice could spend a lifetime just trying to hold it — and still not reach the edges.

So I animate it instead.

She Is African is an anthology series about African people — their lives, their history, their everyday moments, their extraordinary ones. Girls and boys. Women and men. The ancient and the present. Let me tell you about us.

The ones history tried to erase. The ones happening right now in a city that looks a lot like yours. The joyful ones. The heavy ones. The funny ones. The ones that never made it into any history book. The ones your aunty tells at family dinners when she thinks the children are not listening.

I tell stories about girls finding themselves. Boys carrying pressure they never asked for. Mothers loving in the only language they know. Men who built empires. Women who resisted. Children who saw everything.

I travel — through time, through cultures, through the fifty-four countries that make up this continent I call home.

Every episode is a different world. Every world is true.

This is She Is African. Pull up a chair. We are not one story. We are thousands. And I am just getting started. Let me tell you about us.

— Imani, Griot

Who She Is
The girl everyone comes to. She has words for other people's pain, clarity for other people's confusion. Calm on the surface. A private world quietly unraveling underneath.

She gives advice. She does not have answers.
She holds people together. She is quietly unraveling.
She was named Faith. She is still searching for it.
She tells everyone else's stories. She is still writing her own.
